# Application

**Project:** lone-ant

## What is it actually for?

A quiet, low-pressure thing to pick up on a phone — a short session here, a
longer one there — where you slip into the skin of an ant and help a colony
get by. It is the kind of game you return to across days, not one you binge
for hours. Because the colony persists offline, closing the app isn't the end
of a run; it's just the world continuing without you.

## Who is it for?

Hobby game tinkerers — people who like messing with games and game-making
more than they like being "the user" of a polished product. They enjoy the
feeling of a tiny world that exists on its own and that they can nudge,
compare, and tinker with. Even though the game is offline, part of the fun is
watching "your" colony grow and imagining how a friend's turned out.

## Where would it live?

On an Android phone, offline, with no setup. You open it on a commute or
while winding down, do a little, and put it away. No emulator, no studio, no
fuss — the whole thing is meant to be built and played from the phone itself.

## Why does it matter?

Most games ask you to win. This one asks you to *be* something small and part
of something bigger, and to feel that the world kept living after you left.
For the tinkerer, it is also a friendly sandbox: a real, shippable game you
can actually finish with the help of AI tools, rather than a grand idea that
dies half-built.
